Official Biography

Yehia Abouseif is a visual artist, specializing in graphic design, digital product design, and fine art. Twenty-one now, the international student moved to New York City in 2016, studying Communication Design (BFA) at Parsons School of Design. He previously worked for 1on1 Projects, Damian Loeb and J. Walter Thompson, building and prototyping websites, designing visuals and creating mockups. He currently works for Daymond John at The Shark Group, as a Communication Designer. Influences include: Ed Ruscha, the colour red, Yorgos Lanthimos, and anything in between.

Statement

Born and raised in Cairo, Egypt, I was introduced to a particular meaning of art and design and assumed that my influences and interests would be limited to such. It wasn’t until later in my adolescent years when I realised just how untrue that is. While studying at the British International School of Cairo, the education received from English speakers, that of European art; English literature; Western cinema; languages and dialects; and the context of it all, ideologically contrasted with a traditional and middle-eastern upbringing. While being raised by traditional parents came with its own list of benefits – family values, self-determination, ambition and cultural appreciation – the contradicting ways of life encouraged me to question all that was around me, first cynically but then later critically. Once my younger mind was able to move past the feeling of estrangement and indecision, I was able to fully appreciate the privilege of exposure. And as my work still calls onto the simultaneous state of feeling apart of and apart from both worlds, I try to not let that overpower the beauty and chaos that is being portrayed, regardless of what world it derives from.
