Official Biography

Yehia Abouseif is an aspiring graphic designer, screenwriter and artist. Twenty-one now, he moved to New York City in 2016, studies Communication Design (BFA) at Parsons School of Design. He previously worked for 1on1 Projects, Damian Loeb and J. Walter Thompson, building websites, creating mockups, designing menus and brochures, prototyped or comped. Influences include: Ed Ruschua, the colour red, Yorgos Lanthimos and anything in between.

Statement

Born and raised in Cairo, Egypt, I was unwittingly introduced to a specific culture and meaning of art, and assumed my influences were limited to such. It wasn’t until later in my adolescent years when I realised just how untrue that is: while studying at the British International School of Cairo. While being raised by traditional parents had its benefits: family values, self-determination and cultural appreciation, the education received from English speakers, of European art; English literature; Western cinema; languages and dialects; and the context of all; allowed me to reach a transcendent level of perception. One would describe such a level as chaotic — my younger self included — but simultaneously euphoric. Through my practice as an artist, writer or designer, more often than not, the content I produce connects to my self-proclaimed quest for recognized individualism — feeling a part of, yet apart from the universal archetypes of individuality — which is apropos given that my cognitive archival process does not limit me to one region of the world, and neither do my inspirations. My decision to stop embracing the one culture I was born into and omnisciently analyze it instead, strengthened my interest and ability to analyze others: an unprecedented milestone hitherto the dawn of millennials and the true meaning of globalisation.
