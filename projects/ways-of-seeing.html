A digital take on John Berger's Ways of Seeing

John Berger was an English art critic, novelist, painter and poet. John Berger wrote ​Ways of Seeing: a​ 1972 television series that was intended as a response to Kenneth Clark’s ​Civilisation​ (a traditional perspective), and a book that consists of seven numbered essays, four using words and images and three using only images. Both the series and book criticize traditional western cultural aesthetics by raising questions about hidden principles in visual images. Along with Berger, the book was made by Sven Blomberg, Chris Fox, Michael Dibb and Richard Hollis. Those who enjoy the practice of visual storytelling will appreciate the effect of pictorial essays.
