Arabic Courier

While working on my thesis on fictional realities, one of the byproducts includes an Arabic version of the typeface, Courier. Initially created for IBM’s typewriters, Courier is now the standard font used for screenplays. The cinematic world that I am building through props and ephemera – the world of The International – consists of both English and Arabic dialogue, however most Arabic scripts are written in a generic Arabic font and I wanted a script that had a cohesive structure and aesthetic. Moreover, I designed Arabic Courier, a font with similar characteristics to the preexisting Courier, where serifs and counter-forms are interpreted into the Semitic Arabic, and to be used by other Arabic-speaking screenwriters alongside of Howard “Bud” Kettler’s Latin counterpart. Final type specimen will be published shortly.
