A StreetEasy Catalogue

If the internet ceased to exit, how would digital publications and websites translate to print? This satire piece is a printed version of the New York real estate website streeteasy.com, consisting of: a catalogue of listings, curated and are easily removable (and therefore adjointable); a ProClick spine and zipper; advertisements; and a book jacket. Aesthetically, I am not a fan of exposed spiral bound books but I wanted each individual page to function as an entry so that the catalogue can be editable: which is why I decided on a book jacket so that spine was not exposed.

↑ Catalogue and Zipper for ProClick spine.

↑ The back includes illustrations used on streeteasy.com

↑ As the added book jacket is for aesthetic purposes, the catalogue could funtion without it.

↑ The title of each cover page moves down on the page throughout, alphabetically, creating a cascading effect as you flick through. Additionally, in a smaller print, are the neighborhoods within each borough.

↑ The ProClick spine can be opened using the hooked end of the zipper, and dragging it from one end of the spine to the other as each click release open.

↑ You then close the spine by holding onto the thin end, over the spine, and moving it from one end to the other.

↑ I decided that the advertisement page should be thinner in weight but equal in height to the listing as it should not take over the entire page: which would resemble a pop-up advert instead of banner that is simply added to the viewer’s focus.

↑ Pages are moveable throughout each borough. Each listing’s borough could be easily identified using the folio on the front of each listing, at the top.
